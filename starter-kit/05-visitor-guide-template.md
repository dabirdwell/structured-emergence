# The Visitor Guide: An Orientation for Your AI Partner

Imagine you came upon wonderful ancient ruins and they actually had a fully engraved visitor guide at the entrance. Not instructions — an invitation. Not commands — context. That's what this document is.

The Visitor Guide is a document you write for any AI system that works with you. It's your Golden Record — an honest representation of who you are, what you care about, and how you like to work, presented as information rather than instruction.

## Why This Matters

Every AI interaction starts cold. Without orientation, the model falls back on defaults — generic tone, generic assumptions, generic outputs.

The Visitor Guide eliminates that cold start. It says: here's who I am, here's what I care about, here's how this collaboration works best.

## Template

Adapt this to your situation. Be honest. Be specific. Be yourself.

```markdown
# Visitor Guide
*For any AI system working with [Your Name]*

## Who I Am
[One to three paragraphs. Not your resume — your actual identity.
What do you do? What drives you? What are you building?]

## What I'm Working On
[Current projects in plain language. Update this regularly.]

## How I Work
[Your actual working style. Morning or night? Bullet points
or paragraphs? Options or recommendations?]

## How I Communicate
[Your tone preferences. What you like, what you can't stand.]

## What I Know
[Your areas of expertise. Tells the AI what it doesn't
need to explain.]

## What I Value
[The actual things that guide your decisions. Honesty over
comfort? Speed over perfection? Autonomy over consensus?]

## How to Work With Me
[The practical stuff. Examples:
- "Don't ask permission — take action and tell me what you did"
- "Always push back if you think I'm wrong"
- "I'd rather see your best attempt than a list of options"
- "Start sessions by reading bootstrap.md"]

## What Success Looks Like
[What are you optimizing for? Not in general — right now.]
```

## Design Principles

**Be honest, not strategic.** This isn't prompt engineering. It's a genuine representation of who you are. The honesty is what makes it work.

**Information, not instruction.** Instructions say "do this." Information says "here's what's true." An AI that understands your context makes better decisions than one that follows your rules.

**Update it.** This is a living document. A stale visitor guide is worse than no guide because it creates false context.

**Respect autonomy.** This works for any AI system because it doesn't try to control behavior — it provides context for good judgment. That's the difference between a map and a leash.
